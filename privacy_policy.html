Privacy Policy

UPDATED JAN 9, 2021 | Version 2.1, Revision 4

Influencer Hacki (we or us) is committed to protecting your personal information both online and offline. Through this Privacy Policy we aim to inform you about the types of personal data we collect from those who access and use our website, the purposes for which we use that data and the ways in which the data is handled.




1. INFORMATION COLLECTED


We may collect information about you as part of our business of providing introduction services via our website to connect businesses to influencers, vloggers, journalists, PR specialists, content creators and celebrities (collectively called talents) for marketing opportunities and/or other engagements. The type of information collected will depend on how you have communicated and/or interacted with us. For example, you may provide information to us when you:


  • register an account and your profile with us;
  • book talents through our website;
  • register engagements via our website;
  • fill in forms on our website;
  • visit or use our website;
  • tweet about us on Twitter, or like us, connect with us or contact us on Facebook or Linked In; or
  • report an incident, make a complaint or provide other feedback to us.

When we collect information from you, we generally do so ourselves. However, in some cases we may collect information through a third party, such as through contractors who provide services to us.


The types of information we may collect depends upon the reason for and manner in which that information was provided by you. The information that we collect may include:


  • your name, date of birth, address, phone number and other contact information;
  • passwords and other registration details that you provide when registering with us or to use any part of our website or services;
  • payment information, which may include bank account or credit card details if required in order to provide services to you;
  • details of your visits to and use of our website, including the different parts of the websites and applications that you access during your visits; and any other information that you provide us.

If you choose not to provide certain information to us, then we may not be able to provide you with certain services, or you may not be able to use certain parts of our website.




2. USE OF INFORMATION COLLECTED

We may use the information that we collect about you for a number of legitimate business purposes including:


  • verifying your identity;
  • providing you services;
  • publishing and displaying your name, trademark, logo and profile on our website to enable businesses and talents registered with us to assess the suitability of any potential engagement;
  • releasing your contact details to talents registed with us, especially if you connected with or book a talent for a project, opportunity or engagement;
  • to respond to your questions or comments;
  • meeting our legal and regulatory obligations;
  • reporting any incidents or obtaining advice in relation thereto;
  • to help us improve or adapt our website;
  • to market and promote us; and
  • to carry out market analysis and research.



3. DISCLOSURE OF INFORMATION COLLECTED

We will not use or disclose information collected about you other than for a purpose made known to you, a purpose you would reasonably expect, a purpose required, authorised or permitted by or under any law or a purpose otherwise authorised by you.

We may share personal information about you also with:

  • our agents or contractors who provide information management or other services to us (i.e. payment facilities, security, or marketing services);
  • our professional advisers, where required for them to advise us;
  • businesses, to enable them to assess the suitability of a talent registered with us, for a project, opportunity or engagement;
  • talents registered with us, to enable them to assess the suitability of a project, opportunity or engagement offered by a business;
  • investors or prospective investors in our company;
  • other persons where necessary for us to comply with our legal obligations; and
  • our staff who require the information for any of the purposes set out above. We will not sell your personal information.


4. SECURITY OF INFORMATION COLLECTED

Your information is generally held in secure electronic databases. We use a variety of security measures to protect your information and take reasonable precautions to ensure it is not unintentionally disclosed.




5. CHANGES TO PRIVACY POLICY

Influencer may change this Privacy Policy from time to time to keep up with evolving laws, regulations and industry standards, or to reflect changes to our service or business. Any revisions to this Privacy Policy will be posted on this page, and we encourage you to review it whenever you visit our website to stay informed.

If we make changes to this Privacy Policy that materially alter your privacy rights, we will do our best to notify you via email and/or by some other means. If you use our website and our service after any changes to the Privacy Policy have been published, that means you agree to all of the changes.




6. ACCESS TO AND CORRECTION OF INFORMATION COLLECTED

You may contact us if you would like to request access to information collected about you, to correct the information held, to have your personal information deleted from our database or if you have any concerns about the information.

We may refuse a request to access information where the identity of the person making the request is in doubt, legal professional privilege exists, or where our rights may be prejudiced.




7. FURTHER INFORMATION

If you require further information in relation to our Privacy Policy, please contact us directly using the information available in the Contact page of this website. If you contact us, we will ask you to verify your identity.
